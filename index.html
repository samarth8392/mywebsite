Decoding the genetics of cancer, one genome at a time.

I study the genomic underpinnings of cancer and endangered wildlife using multi-omics approaches at the Center for Cancer Research Collaborative Bioinformatics Resource (CCBR), National Cancer Institute / NIH. My work spans whole-genome sequencing, RNA-seq, population genomics, and high-performance computing.

Research Highlights

Genetic Load in Small Populations

Introduced the conceptual distinction between "potential" vs "realized" genetic load — featured in Nature Reviews Genetics. Published in Evolution, winner of SSE Presidents' Award 2024.

2023

Neutral & Functional Genomic Diversity

Population genomic study of Eastern Massasauga rattlesnakes showing correlation between neutral and functional diversity. Published in PNAS.

2023

Snake Fungal Disease Transcriptomics

Multi-tissue RNA-seq study of host immune responses to Ophidiomyces ophidiicola in federally endangered rattlesnakes. Published in Molecular Ecology.
